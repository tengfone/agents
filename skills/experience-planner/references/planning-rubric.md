# Planning Rubric

Use this file when ranking candidates and shaping the final itinerary.

## Candidate Checklist

Capture these fields for each serious candidate:

- `title`
- `category`
- `area`
- `start window`
- `duration`
- `travel_minutes`
- `estimated_cost`
- `booking_required`
- `weather_risk`
- `novelty`
- `trend_signal`
- `source_quality`
- `checked_at`
- `source_url`

Discard weak candidates early if they are closed, sold out, too expensive, require unrealistic travel, or cannot be verified with current sources.

## Search Strategy

Search in this order:

1. Primary activity anchored to the user's window, companions, and area
2. Local trend scan for popular, recently opened, or currently buzzing options in the same zone
3. Nearby food, dessert, coffee, or drinks that match the same area and spend
4. Optional low-commitment follow-up within walking distance or one short ride

Prefer primary sources over listicles. If you use aggregator or map listings, verify the final shortlist on the venue or event page when possible.

## Scoring

Use a simple weighted judgment rather than a rigid formula:

- `fit`: match the user's mood, companions, budget, and constraints
- `availability`: currently open, still bookable, or realistically walk-in friendly
- `proximity`: short travel from the user's starting point and between itinerary legs
- `novelty`: different from recent history without feeling risky for the moment
- `momentum`: local popularity, recent buzz, or signs of current demand without becoming a tourist trap
- `cost`: aligned with the user's budget ceiling and spend tolerance
- `source_quality`: verified by current primary sources and supported by at least one live discovery or demand signal
- `resilience`: still works if weather shifts, a venue is full, or energy drops

Hard penalties:

- clearly closed or sold out
- unverifiable hours, pricing, or event timing
- outdoor-only during bad weather when no fallback exists
- multiple long transfers for a short outing
- strong overlap with the user's recent activity history

## Itinerary Patterns

Use one of these shapes:

- `Quick reset`: one easy activity plus one nearby food or drink stop
- `Date plan`: one focal activity, one meal or dessert stop, one optional scenic or low-pressure follow-up
- `Friends plan`: one social anchor with flexible arrival time, then food or drinks nearby
- `Solo recharge`: one personally satisfying activity with minimal coordination and low travel

## Backup Rules

Backups should differ in at least one of these dimensions:

- indoor versus outdoor
- reservation-heavy versus walk-in friendly
- active versus relaxed
- premium versus budget

Do not present backups that fail for the same reason as the primary plan.
