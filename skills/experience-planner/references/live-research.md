# Live Research

Use this file when the user wants current recommendations, nearby options, or anything that depends on live conditions.

## Core Rule

For time-sensitive recommendations, browse the web during the run. Do not rely on memory for:

- opening hours
- event schedules
- weather
- ticket availability
- current pricing
- "popular now" or "trending nearby" claims

If browsing is unavailable, say the results are not live and switch to either generic suggestions or a request to enable browsing.

## Source Priority

Use sources in this order:

1. Official venue, organizer, ticketing, park, museum, or business pages for hours, schedule, pricing, and booking
2. Map and business listings for reviews, crowd signals, photos, and cross-checking hours
3. Event aggregators and local publishers for discovery, then verify finalists on an official source
4. Recent social or community posts only as supporting evidence for momentum, never as the sole source for time-sensitive facts

## Trend Signals

Treat "trend" as local momentum, not just global popularity. Useful signals include:

- recent openings or newly announced events
- many recent reviews in the area
- highly rated places with fresh review activity
- neighborhood roundups or city guides published recently
- multiple current listings for the same event or class series
- visible booking pressure, limited slots, or timed entry demand

Prefer signals from the last 30 to 60 days unless the category is inherently evergreen.

## Minimum Verification Per Serious Candidate

Capture these before putting a candidate in the final shortlist:

- official or authoritative source URL
- current open hours, session time, or event date
- price or ticketing guidance if relevant
- one live trend signal or demand signal
- rough travel fit for the user's starting area

For very time-sensitive options like classes, events, and pop-ups, verify with at least two distinct sources when possible.

## Search Pattern

Run the research in passes:

1. Broad discovery for the user's area, vibe, and time window
2. Trend scan for "popular", "best rated", "new", "opening", "happening", or equivalent local intent
3. Official verification for the strongest candidates
4. Nearby food or follow-up options in the same area

Gather five to ten candidates quickly, then narrow to the best verified options.

## Freshness Rules

- Weather should be checked for the actual outing day
- Hours and event schedules should be checked during the same session as the recommendation
- Trend sources should be recent enough to reflect current local momentum
- Avoid citing stale listicles or undated pages when fresher alternatives exist

## Failure Modes

Do not present results as live if any of these are true:

- browsing failed or was unavailable
- only stale or low-confidence sources were found
- the venue or event could not be verified as currently open or scheduled
- pricing or booking rules were clearly inconsistent across sources

When that happens, state the limitation in one sentence and either broaden the search or downgrade to non-live suggestions.
